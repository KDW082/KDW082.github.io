March 24, 2026 Astronomy

The James Webb caught something it wasn't supposed to find

There's a galaxy cluster out there — 13.1 billion light years away — that has no business being as structured as it is. The models say it's too early. And yet there it is, sitting in the infrared like a counterargument to everything we thought we knew about the early universe.

"The universe keeps exceeding our imagination of it. That's not a failure of science — that's science working exactly as it should."

What I love about astronomy is that the surprises don't terrify me. They feel like relief. The universe is bigger than my problems, my deadlines, my days. Which it is. Which it always was.

March 22, 2026 Philosophy

Becker was right — we build things so we don't have to think about dying

I've been sitting with Ernest Becker again. The Denial of Death is one of those books that ruins you a little, in the best way. His argument: everything humans create — art, religion, empire, legacy — is a response to the terror of being a mortal animal who knows it's mortal.

"To be fully human is to know you will die, and to create anyway."

The question isn't whether you're running from death. You are. We all are. The question is what you build while you're running.

March 10, 2026 Music

A playlist for driving north on 127 after midnight

There's a specific emotional frequency to leaving Detroit late at night when the road opens up past Flint and the city finally releases its grip. The right music in the right landscape at the right time is as close to transcendence as I've personally managed.

Who this is

Thinker. Designer. Bay City to Mt. Pleasant to Minneapolis to Berkley, Michigan — chasing questions nobody around me seemed bothered by. Philosophy notebooks became a design career. The through-line never changed: the search for knowledge is divinity. I wrote that at twenty. I still believe it.

“Nature is what we know / Yet have no art to say / So impotent Our Wisdom is / To her Simplicity.” — Emily Dickinson. I spent decades trying to say it anyway. Then I started growing things instead. Hot sauce, pesto, holy basil on the Fourth of July. The edible forest is the real curriculum — apples to butter, honey to mead, cedar to candle. The plan, when the time comes, is to tend it full-time and hand things over fences to neighbors.

I asked Jeri Chard to share this lifetime with me at the lighthouse at Old Mission Point. She said yes. We own a chalet near Higgins Lake we love unreasonably. I stand at the water enough mornings to understand what Mary Oliver meant — presence is not passive. It's the whole thing.

Becker called projects like this one immortality projects. He wasn't wrong. I built it eyes open. You're reading it, which means it worked.

The search for knowledge is divinity.
