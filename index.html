Who this is

Thinker. Designer. Bay City to Mt. Pleasant to Minneapolis to Berkley, Michigan — chasing questions nobody around me seemed bothered by. Philosophy notebooks became a design career. The through-line never changed: the search for knowledge is divinity. I wrote that at twenty. I still believe it.

“Nature is what we know / Yet have no art to say / So impotent Our Wisdom is / To her Simplicity.” — Emily Dickinson. I spent decades trying to say it anyway. Then I started growing things instead. Hot sauce, pesto, holy basil on the Fourth of July. The edible forest is the real curriculum — apples to butter, honey to mead, cedar to candle. The plan, when the time comes, is to tend it full-time and hand things over fences to neighbors.

I asked Jeri Chard to share this lifetime with me at the lighthouse at Old Mission Point. She said yes. We own a chalet near Higgins Lake we love unreasonably. I stand at the water enough mornings to understand what Mary Oliver meant — presence is not passive. It's the whole thing.

Becker called projects like this one immortality projects. He wasn't wrong. I built it eyes open. You're reading it, which means it worked.
