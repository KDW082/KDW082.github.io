I still have that old notebook, tucked away in a box somewhere. Flipping through its pages, I came across a poem I wrote at twenty - "Entity". The words "irresistible dimension" and "pure ascension" jump out at me now. I was so caught up in the idea of transcending, of leaving the mundane behind. I thought that's what it meant to be alive.

Now, I'm not so sure. I've spent decades chasing that ascension, trying to tap into something greater. And what have I found? That the search itself is what's divine, not the endpoint. The green-eyed cosmos I once wanted to abandon still has its hold on me, but I see it differently now. It's not something to be transcended, but something to be lived within.

I think about the lines "arbitrarily we endure / departing as a vapor". It's still true - our time here is fleeting, and we're all just trying to make sense of it. But what I've learned is that it's in the enduring, in the everyday moments, that we find meaning. Not in some grand, celestial plan. I'm not sure if my younger self would understand, but I'm okay with that. The search continues, and that's enough.
