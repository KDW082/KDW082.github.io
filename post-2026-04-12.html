The sunlight cuts through the water at Higgins Lake, illuminating the smooth stones on the lake floor. I wade in, feeling the chill of the morning water, and dive down to touch the bottom. The clarity is almost unsettling, like I'm floating in a dream. As I swim back to the surface, I see Jeri sitting on the dock, reading a book. Barb and Mable, our calico sisters, are sprawled out beside her, soaking up the sun.

I climb out of the water and sit down next to Jeri, watching the way the light dances across the ripples on the lake. We sit in comfortable silence, the only sound the occasional bird calling out or a fish breaking the surface. It's moments like these that I'm reminded of the complexity of human emotions. I think about the times I've laughed at someone's misfortune, or felt a deep sadness at another's loss. But as I sit here, surrounded by the stillness of nature, I'm struck by the desire to simply understand.

The water laps gently against the shore, a soothing rhythm that seems to echo the beat of my own heart. I look over at Jeri, and see the depth of her own thoughts reflected in her eyes. In this moment, I'm aware of the tension between my desire for control and the randomness of life. The lake, with its crystal-clear water, seems to embody this paradox – its beauty is both serene and unsettling. As I sit here, I feel the weight of my own mortality, and the impermanence of everything around me. The universe stretches out before me, vast and mysterious, and I'm left with only the gentle lapping of the water against the shore.
