The crunch of a fresh jalapeño between my teeth, the smell of holy basil wafting from the cutting board. I'm making cabin salsa, the same recipe Jeri and I have perfected over the years. The garden's been kind to us this season, and the harvest is overflowing. I chop, I dice, I mix, lost in the rhythm of preservation. The thought of winter months, of cold mornings by the lake, makes every jar a promise of warmth to come.

As I work, my mind starts to wander. I think of all the times we've driven up to the cabin, the city's grip loosening with each mile. The freedom of those trips, the feeling of being untethered, it's addictive. I crave that madness, that desire for everything at once. To be alive, to be present, to be making something with my own hands – it's all connected.

In the stillness of the cabin, surrounded by the quiet of the forest, I feel most alive. It's a feeling that's hard to put into words, but it's like the universe is humming in tune with my own wild heart. The jars of salsa, the pickles, they're not just food – they're a testament to the beauty of being fully engaged, fully alive. And when I'm gone, maybe these small creations will remain, a whisper of the madness that drove me to live, to love, to make.
