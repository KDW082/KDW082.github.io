As I sit on the Berkley deck, surrounded by the quiet of the morning, I notice the way the sunlight filters through the trees, casting dappled shadows on the wood. It's a fleeting moment, one that I've tried to capture in my design work, but never quite managed to replicate. The hierarchy of elements, the balance of light and dark, it's all so delicate. I think about how most designers are afraid to commit to hierarchy, to make a statement about what matters.

In my own life, I've struggled with this too. Jeri often tells me that I'm quieter than I need to be, that my thoughts are more nuanced than I let on. It's a tension I've lived with for a long time - the desire to be myself, to let my true thoughts and feelings emerge, versus the fear of being overwhelmed by the expectations of others. As I sit here, watching the sunlight dance across the deck, I feel a sense of resolve. It's not about creating a perfect design, or saying the right thing, it's about embracing the imperfect, the uncertain.

In this moment, I feel a sense of connection to something larger than myself. The stillness of the morning, the beauty of the natural world, it's all a reminder that life is short, and unstable. And yet, it's in these moments of quiet reflection that I find my own sense of purpose, my own sense of self. The world may try to shape me into something else, but in these moments, I am free to simply be.
