I was out in the garden the other day, harvesting holy basil for pesto. The sun was high, and the smell of the earth and leaves filled my lungs. I started thinking about all the systems I've designed over the years - the gardens, the hot sauce recipes, the brewing process. I've optimized them, tweaked them, and controlled for variables. They work, most of the time. But as I was washing the dirt off my hands, I caught myself wondering if Jeri would like the pesto, if it would make her happy. And that's when it hit me - all these systems, all this knowledge, it's all just a means to an end. The end being a good life, a life well-lived.

I thought about all the books I've read, the philosophies I've studied, the conversations I've had. Everyone's got an answer, a solution, a path to follow. But the more I learn, the more I realize that the only question that really matters is the one I've been avoiding - how to live. Not how to make a living, or how to be happy, but how to live. With all the knowledge and wisdom in the world, we still haven't figured that one out. And maybe that's what makes it so beautiful - the not knowing, the searching, the trying.
