I still think about those lines I wrote at twenty - "train bridge tombstone / concrete plate tectonics / I comprehend life, Every moment unstable". I remember scribbling them in a notebook, feeling like I'd cracked the code. Life was unpredictable, and that was the only constant.

Now, I'm not sure I was wrong, but I am sure it's more complicated. I've spent years designing, building, and trying to impose order on chaos. And yet, the more I've learned, the more I've come to realize that instability isn't something to be solved, it's just the way things are.

I've had my share of successes and failures, and through it all, I've tried to stay curious, to keep asking questions. The search for knowledge is still what drives me, what gives me a sense of purpose. But I've also learned to appreciate the beauty in the uncertainty, to find comfort in the unknown.

It's funny, I used to think that if I could just understand the world, I could control it. Now, I know that's not how it works. The best I can do is show up, pay attention, and try to make sense of things as they happen. And even that's not always possible. But it's in those moments, when I'm fully present, that I feel most alive.
