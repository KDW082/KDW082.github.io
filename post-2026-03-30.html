I was driving north on 127 the other night, past Bay City, and the darkness felt like a reminder that we're still just scratching the surface of what's out there. I'd been reading about this galaxy cluster, 13.1 billion light years away, that's got scientists puzzled. It's too organized, too structured, for its age. The models say it shouldn't be that way, but there it is.

It got me thinking about the times in my life when I felt like I was ahead of schedule, like I'd stumbled into something that didn't quite add up. Meeting Jeri, landing my first design job, building a life that felt like home – none of it was supposed to happen the way it did. But it did.

Maybe that's the thing about being alive: we're all just galaxy clusters, defying the models and expectations. We're messy, unstructured, and yet somehow we come together into something beautiful. It's not always easy, and it's not always explainable, but it's real.

I've been brewing a batch of mead in the backyard, using apples from the trees out back. It's a slow process, but the end result is worth it. It's like life, I suppose – unpredictable, messy, and full of unexpected beauty. And when I sit here, sipping on that mead, listening to Shannon Hoon's voice, I'm reminded that sometimes the things that don't make sense are the most beautiful of all.
