I was driving north on 127 after midnight, the only sound the hum of the engine and the occasional passing truck. The darkness outside was almost palpable, a physical presence that pressed in on the car. It was then that I felt it - a sense of release, of shedding the weights that we accumulate in our daily lives. The weights of right and wrong, of good and bad, of expectation and obligation.

As I drove, the trees and fields blurred together, becoming a single, undifferentiated expanse. It was as if the world had been reduced to its essence, stripped of the labels and categories that we use to make sense of it. In that moment, I felt a deep sense of connection to the land, to the universe, and to the people in my life.

I thought of Jeri, and the way she smiles when she's happy. I thought of the way the light falls on Higgins Lake in the morning, and the smell of the forest after a rain. It was as if all of these things were connected, linked by a thread that ran deeper than our everyday concerns. And in that moment, I knew that I would always strive to meet her, to meet myself, and to meet the world, in that field beyond the boundaries of our making.
