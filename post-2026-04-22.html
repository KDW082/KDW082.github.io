I've been watching the way the morning sun catches the power lines that run along 127, just north of the chalet. The lines themselves are nothing special, but the way the sunlight reflects off the thin wires creates a kind of shimmering effect, like the air is vibrating just above them. It's as if the light is being pulled apart and put back together again, right there in the space between the wire and the sky. I've tried to take a picture of it, but it never translates - the camera just captures a dull, grey line suspended between two poles. It's only when you're standing there, in the cold, early morning air, that you can see it. And even then, it's not something you can really look at - it's more like something you notice out of the corner of your eye, when you're not paying attention.

I was thinking about this the other day, when I was talking to Jeri about her work. She was saying how sometimes, when she's designing a system, she'll get so focused on the individual components that she forgets about the way they interact with each other. And I think that's what's happening with those power lines - the individual wires, the poles, the sunlight - they're all just doing their own thing, but when you put them together, something else emerges. Something that's not really about any of those individual parts, but about the space between them. And that's what I think we're always trying to capture, in our designs, in our relationships, in our lives - that shimmering effect, that vibration in the air. But it's slippery, and it's easy to miss, and sometimes I think the only way to really see it is to stop looking, and just notice what's there, out of the corner of your eye.
