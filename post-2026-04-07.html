I woke up before dawn, the darkness outside my window a reminder that some things are still beyond my control. The fire was out, but the ashes were warm, so I added some kindling and watched as the flames slowly came back to life. It's a small, mundane task, but it's one that requires attention and patience.

As I sat there, waiting for the coffee to brew, I couldn't help but think about the ways we spend our time trying to define what's good, what's right, and what's just. We argue and debate, trying to convince others of our point of view, when in reality, the only thing that truly matters is what we do with our own two hands.

I think about Jeri, and how she always knows how to make me see the beauty in the everyday. She's the one who taught me that happiness is something you can design, that it's not just some fleeting feeling, but a choice you make every morning. And I think about my own struggles, about the times I've gotten caught up in trying to prove myself, to show others that I'm worthy. But in the end, it's not about what others think, it's about what I do, and how I live my life.

The flames are dancing now, casting a warm glow over the room. It's a small, peaceful moment, one that reminds me that the only way to truly be a good person is to simply be one, in every moment, no matter what.
