I was out in the garden this morning, tending to the holy basil, when I stumbled upon a small, intricately woven spider web. It was like a tiny, dew-kissed cathedral, suspended between two stalks of basil. I stood there, frozen, taking it in. The way the light danced through it, the delicate threads that held it together. It was a reminder that, even in the smallest of things, there's beauty to be found.

I think that's what it means to pay attention - to slow down enough to notice the tiny miracles that surround us. Not just the grand, sweeping vistas, but the small, quiet moments that make up the bulk of our lives. The way the sunlight filters through the leaves, the sound of the wind rustling through the trees, the smell of the earth after a spring rain.

I've been trying to cultivate this sense of attention, to be more present in my daily life. It's not always easy - my mind wanders, I get caught up in distractions. But when I do manage to focus, to really pay attention, I'm always amazed at what I find. The world is a vast and wondrous place, full of beauty and mystery. And it's only when I slow down, and really look, that I begin to see it.
