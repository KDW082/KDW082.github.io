I think about those nights driving north on 127, the only sound the hum of the engine and the occasional passing truck. Jeri's hand on my knee, the darkness of the road ahead. We'd talk about everything and nothing, the words spilling out like we were trying to fill the void between us and the stars. It was like we were trying to consume the whole world, to taste every flavor, to feel every sensation.

Those were the nights I felt most alive, most connected to the people and the place. It wasn't about the destination; it was about the movement, the momentum. We'd stop at some diner in the middle of nowhere, and the coffee would be bad, but the conversation would be electric. We'd talk about our dreams, our fears, our desires. The world would feel like it was ours for the taking.

It's funny, because when I look back, those nights weren't about being reckless or carefree. They were about being present, about being fully engaged with the moment. We weren't worried about what came next or what we'd left behind. We were just there, in the car, in the dark, with each other. And that's what I think Kerouac was getting at – that desire to be fully alive, to experience everything, to feel the rush of life in all its beauty and ugliness.
